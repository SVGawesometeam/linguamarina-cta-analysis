Executive Playbook — What to Do in Your Next Video

Based on $57,935 in verified product revenue across 133 campaigns, Jan 2025 – Apr 2026. TOEFL content delivers 4× higher revenue per customer than General English content.
1 · What's working
TOEFL content converts at 4× the average order value ($100 vs $25). The top 3 TOEFL campaigns — toeflin2weeks, toeflspeaking, ielts030124 — generate $11,731 combined from only 10,682 sessions.

Grammar book (prepositions video) is a volume machine: 511 products sold, $4,989 revenue, driven by a 15,875-session video with a 23% integration placement and organic topic pivot.

"By the way" opener → product description → promo code → link is the pattern in all high-converting integrations.
2 · What's NOT working
638 campaigns had sessions but $0 revenue. Top wasted: eduintheus_171221 (756 sessions, $0), shadowing (575 sessions, $0).

Very late integrations (>75%) consistently underperform — viewers already got the value and are leaving. toeflupdate_140126: integration at 82%, only 3 sales despite 977 high-intent sessions.

Very early integrations (<10%) also fail — trust hasn't been built yet. toeflcompilation_030925: integration at ~5%, minimal description, zero conversions from intro audience.
3 · What to do in your next video
1. Make a TOEFL video (study plan, score reveal, section tips, or "what I'd do differently").
2. Place the integration at 25–45% of video duration — after the first major teaching point.
3. Run the integration for 60–120 seconds (not 10 seconds).
4. Use a promo code. Always.
4 · What product to promote
TOEFL content → TOEFL course + LinguaTOEFL platform. Avg check: $100–$122. Best performers include it as a bundle (platform = free value, course = paid upgrade).

General grammar/English content → Intermediate to Advanced (i2a) course. Avg check $87.77, 41 sold from one campaign. Strong week-by-week structure resonates.

Basic grammar content → Grammar book. Low price ($9.76 avg) but very high volume if the video has high traffic.
5 · How to promote it (integration structure)
  1. Topic bridge: "By the way, speaking of [content topic]…"
  2. Product intro: Name the product, 1 sentence max
  3. What's inside: 2–3 concrete features (templates, sections, platform)
  4. Social proof: Score, student result, or your own experience
  5. Specific offer: Promo code + discount amount (e.g. LINGUAMARINA = $20 off)
  6. CTA: "Link in description" — one time, clear, not repeated
6 · When in the video
25–45% of video duration. After the first major teaching block, before viewers get everything they came for.

For a 10-minute video: integration at 2:30–4:30
For a 15-minute video: integration at 3:45–6:45
For a 30-minute video: integration at 7:30–13:30

Name it as a YouTube chapter (e.g. "About the TOEFL course") — this makes it trackable and signals transparency to viewers.
7 · What transition to use
"By the way" is the highest-converting opener across all analyzed videos.

It signals a conversational aside — not a hard sell. It feels like Marina is sharing something helpful, not interrupting the lesson.

Alternative confirmed openers:
· "Speaking of [content topic]…"
· Organic chapter transition (chapter title IS the product pitch)
· Personal story lead-in: "I took the TOEFL and here's what I used…"
8–9 · Include review, price, or something else?
Always include a promo code — this is the single biggest differentiator. Videos with promo codes average 2,869/video vs $703/video without.

Social proof is essential for TOEFL — a specific score (119/120, 100+) dramatically increases credibility. For General English, a personal story works instead.

Price or discount %: yes — either state the price or the discount (66% off, $20 off). Vague "great value" language doesn't convert.

Product review: yes for General English, optional for TOEFL — for TOEFL, the course is understood. For grammar/i2a, describing what the product covers is needed.

What converts in TOEFL videos

✓ Specific score / social proof — "Anastasia scored 119/120", "I scored 100+". Score credibility is the main purchase driver for test-prep.
✓ Platform + course bundle — LinguaTOEFL platform as "free" with paid course creates strong perceived value. Reduces barrier to enrollment.

The "late placement paradox" explained:
Some late-placement videos show sales because: (1) the audience is extremely high-intent (TOEFL students who watched the whole video are the most motivated buyers), (2) late in a short video (5-8 min) still means a reasonable absolute timestamp — 75% of 5 minutes is only 3:45, (3) one high-ticket sale ($167–$312) skews the revenue number.

The correct question isn't "did it sell?" but "how many did it miss?" A late placement in a high-traffic TOEFL video with 2,000+ sessions that converts 1–3 sales could have converted 10–20+ if placed at 30–40%.

The Winning Integration Formula

✓ High-Converting Structure (3 confirmed examples)
1
Natural bridge from content — "By the way, speaking of grammar…" Not a jarring interruption. Flows from what Marina just taught.
2
Product name + one-line description — Name it, say what it is. "Grammar Is All You Need" / "TOEFL speaking & writing templates".
3
2–3 specifics of what's inside — Templates for each section / exercises / week-by-week structure. Not vague "everything you need".
4
Credibility anchor — Score (119/120), personal experience ("I used this"), or student result. One concrete number or story.
5
Specific offer — Promo code + exact discount ($20 off / 66% off). Creates urgency and a memorable action item.
6
Single clear CTA — "Link in description." Once. Not repeated. Not a begging tone.
Total block: 60–130 seconds. Placed at 25–45% of video.
✗ Low-Converting Patterns (observed in zero/near-zero sales videos)
✗
Minimal integration — "Check out our TOEFL course, link below." No description, no offer, no social proof. Viewer has no reason to click.
✗
Urgency-only framing — "TOEFL is changing, you need to prepare NOW." Fear without a clear, described solution doesn't convert — it just stresses people.
✗
Consultation-first offer — Leading with "schedule a consultation" before building product value feels like a high-commitment ask too early.
✗
No promo code — Integrations without a promo code average significantly less revenue per video. The code creates a psychological commitment moment.
✗
End-of-video-only — Placing integration at 80–95% means <20% of viewers hear it. Even a great pitch fails with no audience.
